\section{Abstract}
% -------------------------------------------------------
The development of scientific discussion is both a practical question about a research area and a test of text analysis methods. This study examines how AI, machine learning, and NLP research summaries shifted from focusing on theory to practical use, how they altered how they describe limitations, and how the spread of new terms accelerated over the last thirty years. To study these changes, five methods were applied to 24,677 arXiv summaries: keyword analysis, topic modeling (LDA and NMF), classification of how limitations are described, tracking how terms spread (TF-IDF changes, Word2Vec, and SPECTER2), and dimension reduction (PCA and UMAP). Each method provides independent evidence, and all point to 2014–2016 as the main turning point. For example, TF-IDF and Sentence-BERT (all-MiniLM-L6-v2) centroid distance series have a Pearson correlation of $r = 0.863$. Important results show that language focused on applications passed theory-focused language around 2014–2015; ethics-related terms grew from almost none to about 13\% of summaries by 2020–2024; and UMAP suggests the field shifted to new approaches rather than replacing old ones. Also, variation within each year drops significantly (Kendall’s $\tau \approx -0.5$, showing the field is becoming more consistent as it develops. Most importantly, the main way terms change is by quietly shifting meaning rather than by being used more often: 13 of 36 tracked terms changed meaning without a matching increase in use, a result that frequency-based methods alone would miss.
